As a FORTRAN programmer, I have no idea what you mean by classes or object-oriented programming. So I am clueless when you say "assemble classes". I think in terms of physical items. An object to me is a bolt, a nut, a rivet, a wing, an aircraft. So if you know of a way to break down this tremendous language barrier, aside from attempting to develop a FORTRAN API, then I would really like to know. Otherwise, I'm left with no other choice than to build on what I know and understand to something new. I need a foundation, and FORTRAN is it when it comes to writing computer codes. Actually, I began my coding career by writing macro assembler code for an old Z-80 and on the TI-59 where I was using memory registers to hold other memory locations to do array processing. 

>From macro assembler I learned FORTRAN. From FORTRAN I learned Basic and C-Shell and TCL programming. I've even written my own grid manipulation language. But to simply pick up an entirely different set of terms from a language where I have never had any exposure, makes it extremely difficult for me to move forward and use your software.
