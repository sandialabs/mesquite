On 10/16/2012 09:20 AM, Day, David wrote:
> Shang Feifei,
> In my experience, Mesquite moves the nodes that are free.  In particular a boundary node that is free (compare vtk
> format spec for example) moves.  --David
>

But the node doesn't necessarily stay on the boundary.  Allen Kelly presented a paper at this year's IMR where he 
described modifications to Mesquite to account for moving nodes on boundary geometry (and keeping them on the geometry), 
1d and 2d geometric entities both.  We talked to Pat Knupp about getting these into Mesquite code base so others could 
use them, and Pat said they'd take a look at the changes (and Allen committed to sending them to Pat, after he made sure 
they were clean and ready for prime time).  So, this is my way of (hopefully gently...) prodding the Mesquite team to 
make sure this happens ('cuz I could really use it too :) ).
