>    Hi, sorry to chime in late.
>
>    As most of you know, Mesquite has call-back functions which tell the
>code
>    to snap vertices back onto the geometry, provided that geometry (the
>surface normal
>    in particular) has been defined.   This capability allows one to let
>vertices that
>    are on the boundary, but FREE, to move in a reasonable manner.  This
>algorithm is 
>    an approximation to
>    doing the problem 'correctly' via Lagrange multipliers or parametrized
>surfaces.
>    The approximation is usually pretty good unless the geometry curves
>strongly.
>
>    That said, Brian has been working for some time on an alternative
>approach which, 
>    I believe, derives the geometry from the initial mesh.  I'm not sure
>if that is
>    a capability which has been checked into Mesquite, whether or not
>checking it
>    in even makes any sense, and whether or not Brian is willing to do so.
>
>    We did also speak with Allen Kelly about incorporating his method for
>moving nodes
>    on the boundary.   It would be good if these two approaches could be
>compared to 
>    see if they are similar enough that only one need be checked in or if
>there is a
>    benefit to having both.   Please keep me appraised if such a
>comparison is made.
>
>    Thanks,
>      Pat
